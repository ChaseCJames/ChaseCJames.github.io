Modeling & Evolution · Current Work (USC Levine Lab)

ecoTRACE — Phytoplankton Quota Model with Stochastic Evolution

At the University of Southern California in the Levine Lab, I am developing ecoTRACE, a trait-based phytoplankton population dynamics model built on a cellular quota framework and explicitly capable of stochastic evolution. ecoTRACE integrates flexible cellular stoichiometry (e.g., N:C and Chl:C quotas), carbon allocation strategies, and evolving life-history traits within a multi-trait adaptive landscape.

The goal is to understand how physiological plasticity and heritable evolution interact to reshape trait tradeoffs, population dynamics, and ecosystem function under environmental variability. By combining mechanistic physiology with stochastic evolutionary dynamics, ecoTRACE aims to bridge the gap between short-term acclimation and long-term adaptation in marine phytoplankton.

In parallel, I contributed to the development of a genome-scale metabolic modeling framework to classify marine heterotrophic bacteria by functional metabolic strategy. Using thousands of genome-scale metabolic models, we identified eight emergent metabolic guilds — spanning fast-growing generalists to slow-growing specialists — providing a functional basis for linking microbial diversity to biogeochemical processes at regional and global scales.

Marine Microbiology · Biogeography

Microbial Biogeography of the California Current

Microbial communities in the ocean play a foundational role in global biogeochemical cycles, yet the drivers of their spatial and temporal structure remain poorly understood at large scales. As part of the NOAA CalCOFI Ocean Genomics (NCOG) project, I used amplicon sequencing and metagenomics from quarterly cruises spanning the Southern California Current to characterize how nutrient supply and oceanographic gradients structure the marine microbiome.

A central finding of this work, published in Nature Communications (2022), is that nitrate supply — a key driver of primary productivity — is a dominant predictor of microbiome biodiversity and community composition across the upwelling system. I also examined the biogeographic affinities of individual taxa, identifying endemic, cosmopolitan, and habitat-generalist microbes and their distinct ecological niches within the coastal marine environment (Scientific Reports, 2024).

Fisheries Science · Causal Inference

Fisheries Ecology & Applied Marine Science

Prior to focusing on microbial ecology, I worked extensively on fisheries science and ecosystem-based management questions with the Sugihara Lab at Scripps and as a Analytics Lead at MarFishEco. This work applied empirical dynamic modeling, time series analysis, and causal inference to pressing fisheries management problems.

I contributed to studies on the design of incentive systems for reducing Chinook salmon bycatch in the Bering Sea, long-term ecological monitoring frameworks, and a methodological critique of circularity in fisheries data that can artificially inflate predictive performance metrics.
